Good morning. Japan has delivered its most memorable Olympic moment yet at the Milan-Cortina Games, with figure skaters Miura Riku and Kihara Ryuichi [winning gold](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260217/k10015053671000.html) in pairs skating - the country's first-ever medal in this discipline. The "RikuRyu" pair staged a dramatic comeback in the free skate portion after struggling in the short program, setting a new world record score to claim the top spot. Japan now has [18 total medals](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260217/k10015053681000.html), matching its record haul from the Beijing Winter Olympics.

The Olympic high comes as Prime Minister Takaichi faces her first major economic test since taking office. Japan's Q4 GDP grew just [0.2% annualized](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMirgFBVV95cUxOb2lWRkdjVkdqVlg0aVZFVDd1OThNV1p1YzV5Z1JkQS1QQVBoam5pcEJEenhoOVdweWE4dTRMVkIyYkdWbWplMTJkSHBzWVpyTTNSZ0x5UlRWYlctdkVtbDlRNzRWYlNFQXBtd01mdUdJc2FIZmZXb2tCV3NFOEc4UGVaMnBHeDh5Qm0yNEhKUlpxVE5lMEhUbXlqQ0FqZjZCa2tEN3EwSVV6ME1MMkE?oc=5), well below the 1.2% forecast, marking a fragile recovery that Reuters notes poses [early challenges](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiowFBVV95cUxPTkdXZXlRTF8waXBnWEZuY3A4TFJ5cFNCRG9QYmxIR1JiZVB5UzV2ZVVyeGxqUkdqVEZXYTNtTG1UOGo0dFFkdHlkMmtMc0hCdUVjcy1aelk3U3hxalhzSmh2YktZMVY1VURqNVA1d1ZVZE1xUmRHZy1jYjlxaVYxMUFXamxrTkM1V0RfWW13M3g0dzNnc3Vfc0JSVEhvQWJpM25V?oc=5) for the new administration.
