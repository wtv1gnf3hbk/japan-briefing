Japan is scrambling to manage fallout from escalating Middle East tensions as oil prices surge past $100 per barrel and markets plunge. Prime Minister Takaichi told Reuters that Japan is [considering steps](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/japan-considering-steps-cushion-economy-iran-conflict-pm-takaichi-says-2026-03-09/) to cushion the economy from the Iran conflict, while her government evacuated 388 Japanese nationals from the region on two charter flights this weekend. The evacuations underscore how quickly the crisis has escalated — just days ago, these were considered safe transit countries for Japanese travelers and business personnel.

The timing could hardly be worse for Japan's economic recovery. Bloomberg reports that Japan faces [mounting stagflation risk](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/japan-s-stagflation-risk-mounts-with-100-oil-and-sagging-yen) with the oil spike and weakening yen, while stock fear gauges have soared as the crisis upends the bull market scenario that had powered Japanese equities higher this year. The G7 held emergency talks on coordinated oil reserve releases, with Kyodo reporting that the IEA called for [joint release](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/71948) of emergency oil stocks during the meeting.

**Business & Markets**

• Oil prices whipsawed from nearly $120 back below $90 during volatile trading, according to AP, as markets struggled to price in the Iran crisis risks while US stocks ultimately [erased early losses](https://apnews.com/article/oil-stock-markets-iran-war-458890210407eb0cba85c7e1a684c890).

• Japan Display may partner with the US for a new American plant under an investment package, Reuters reports, citing sources who say the deal could be part of broader US-Japan [semiconductor cooperation](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/us-japan-may-partner-with-japan-display-new-us-plant-under-investment-package-2026-03-09/).

• A former Google AI researcher has established a robotics startup in Tokyo, per Bloomberg, aiming to [transform Japan's robots](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/ex-google-researcher-seeks-to-transform-japan-s-robots-with-ai) with artificial intelligence capabilities.

• Japan and Canada signed a strategic agreement covering defense and energy cooperation, AP reports, as the Middle East war [fuels oil concerns](https://apnews.com/article/japan-canada-defense-economic-security-6498f3fe3d3a4804f8ce0165b4eebd51) and both countries seek to diversify supply chains.

**Government & Policy**

• Japan will deploy its first homegrown long-range missiles on March 31 in Kumamoto Prefecture, the Japan Times reports, marking the [first deployment](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/09/japan/japan-deploy-missiles-kumamoto/) of domestically developed standoff weapons as China tensions persist.

• Japan urged Iran to exercise restraint in its conflict with the US and Israel, with Kyodo reporting that Tokyo specifically asked Iran not to [threaten maritime safety](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/71942) in the Strait of Hormuz shipping lane.

• China condemned Taiwan Premier's visit to Japan as harboring "evil designs," according to both Bloomberg and Bloomberg, marking the [first such visit](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/china-warns-japan-over-taiwan-premier-s-first-visit-since-1972) by a Taiwan premier since 1972.

**Coverage Flags**

International outlets are leading on Japan's economic vulnerability to the Iran crisis, with Bloomberg's stagflation analysis and Reuters' government response getting more detailed coverage than Japanese outlets so far. The Taiwan premier visit story also appears to be getting more attention in international press than domestic coverage suggests.
