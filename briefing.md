Japan faces mounting pressure from multiple fronts today as the new U.S. administration [launches trade](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72105) investigations targeting Tokyo and over a dozen other countries. The probes, part of the Trump administration's effort to rebuild tariff pressure after Supreme Court setbacks, come as Japan grapples with [surging oil prices](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/can-japan-s-oil-and-gas-stockpiles-weather-a-middle-east-crisis) following Iran's escalation in the Middle East. Prime Minister Takaichi confirmed Japan will not [send minesweepers](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/12/japan/politics/takaichi-no-sdf-minesweepers-in-middle-east/) to the region despite the maritime crisis.

The economic implications are already visible in Tokyo markets, where [foreign investors](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/foreigners-sell-most-net-japan-stocks-futures-since-november) sold the most Japanese stocks and futures since November. The yen is [approaching](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/yen-nears-year-s-low-as-strategists-see-higher-intervention-bar) its yearly low as strategists see a higher bar for currency intervention. .

**Business & Markets**

• Honda projects up to [690 billion yen](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72156) in net losses this fiscal year, marking its first annual deficit since listing, according to Kyodo News. The company faces a $15.7 billion charge related to its EV strategy review, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/honda-expects-fy202526-loss-up-43-bln-review-ev-strategy-2026-03-12/).

• SoftBank-backed PayPay [raised $879.8 million](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/paypay-softbank-raise-879-8-million-in-payments-firm-s-us-ipo) in its Nasdaq debut, with shares jumping to value the digital payments firm at $12.7 billion, Bloomberg reports.

• Foreign investors dumped Japanese assets in their [biggest selling spree](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/foreigners-sell-most-net-japan-stocks-futures-since-november) since November, as Middle East tensions and U.S. trade pressure weigh on market sentiment.

• The yen weakened toward yearly lows as Bloomberg [analysts suggest](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/yen-nears-year-s-low-as-strategists-see-higher-intervention-bar) authorities may tolerate further declines before intervening, despite rising import costs from oil price spikes.

**Government & Policy**

• Japan and the U.S. agreed to [expand classified](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72175) information sharing in defense cooperation, Kyodo News reports, as security ties deepen following recent diplomatic engagements.

• Prime Minister Takaichi's [approval rating](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/12/japan/takaichi-cabinet-approval-poll/) dropped to 59% in the latest poll, the Japan Times reports, as her government faces pressure over budget battles and Middle East policy.

**What to Watch**

• The ruling coalition plans to push through the fiscal 2026 budget in the Lower House today, [NHK reports](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260313/k10015074231000.html), setting up intensified political confrontation with opposition parties as economic pressures mount.

**Coverage Flags**

International outlets are providing more detailed coverage of Honda's EV strategy crisis and its global implications, while Japanese media focus more on domestic budget politics. The coordination between Japan-U.S. defense information sharing deserves deeper exploration given the timing with Middle East tensions.
