Japan is positioning itself cautiously as tensions escalate between the United States and Iran over the critical Hormuz Strait. Defense Minister Yasukazu Hamada suggested Tokyo might consider [sending Self-Defense Forces](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72754) for minesweeping operations in the strategic waterway, but only after a ceasefire is reached, according to Kyodo News. Bloomberg reports Japan has ruled out [unilateral talks](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-22/japan-says-not-considering-unilateral-talks-with-iran-on-hormuz) with Iran about the blockade, even as Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi wraps up her Washington summit with President Trump.

The diplomatic balancing act comes as Iran has effectively closed the strait to what it calls "enemy-linked" ships following escalating attacks on nuclear facilities. Reuters reports the [Hormuz closure](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/trump-threatens-iran-with-power-plant-strikes-over-hormuz-blockade-2026-03-22) is forcing global importers to scramble for alternatives to the vital energy chokepoint. Meanwhile, AP reports a [Japanese national](https://apnews.com/article/japan-iran-war-detention-release-193f389a5c1fbbcf6c17ff4afeb07d17) detained in Iran last year has been released, providing a rare positive development between the two nations.

**Business & Markets**

• The Japan Times reports that [Kashiwazaki-Kariwa nuclear plant](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/22/japan/kashiwazaki-kariwa-resumes-power-generation/) has resumed power generation, marking a significant step in Japan's energy security strategy during the Middle East crisis.

• According to the Japan Times, average [cherry blossom viewing](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/22/japan/japan-cherry-blossom-viewing-budget-falls/) budgets have fallen 13.8%.

• Bloomberg reports that [war shockwaves](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-21/shockwave-of-war-is-rippling-through-the-global-economy) are rippling through the global economy, with energy markets particularly affected by the Hormuz situation.

• China is pledging more balanced trade following its record surplus, with Premier Li promising national treatment for foreign companies, Reuters reports.

**Government & Policy**

• NHK reports the ruling party is [consulting with](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260323/k10015081611000.html) the government on handling the provisional budget bill, considering the prime minister's intentions as deliberations continue.

• Prime Minister Takaichi posted on X that she completed her Washington meetings and confirmed "a concrete path toward further strengthening the Japan-U.S. alliance," following a dinner where the military band played X Japan's "Rusty Nail."

**What to Watch**

• The ruling party's budget deliberations will be closely watched as Japan navigates the economic fallout from the Middle East conflict while managing domestic fiscal priorities.
