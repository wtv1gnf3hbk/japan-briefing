This reversal puts Japan's automakers in an interesting position — while domestic climate policies remain intact, export opportunities to a deregulated US market could expand.

The policy shift comes as [Japanese officials](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260213/k10015050391000.html) continue detailed discussions over the massive $550 billion investment package agreed with Washington. Economic Industry Minister Akazawa met with Commerce Secretary Ratnic to finalize selection criteria for the first wave of projects in this 80 trillion yen commitment, showing how Japan navigates the new administration's priorities while maintaining its own industrial strategy.
