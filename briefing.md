Oil prices whipped markets across Asia today as the Middle East conflict drove crude briefly above $120 before plunging back below $90. The [G7 agreed](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/business/economy/20260310-315623/) to support global energy supplies following attacks on Iran, while a second Japanese [government-chartered flight](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/politics/politics-government/20260310-315638/) returned 281 nationals from the Middle East to Narita Airport. The volatility underscored how quickly regional tensions can ripple through Japan's import-dependent economy.

Meanwhile, Japan [upgraded its Q4 GDP](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/japan-upgrades-q4-gdp-robust-business-investment-2026-03-10/) to an annualized 1.3% expansion, driven by robust business investment. The revision comes as Prime Minister Takaichi pushes her [investment strategy](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/03/10/japan-investment-targets-ai-quantum-drones/) targeting AI, quantum computing and drones, with the government presenting specific roadmaps for 17 strategic areas at today's Japan Growth Strategy Conference.
