Good morning, Javier. The standoff over the Strait of Hormuz is creating significant pressure on Japan, with President Trump postponing his planned China visit to focus on building a coalition to reopen the waterway. Prime Minister Takaichi [told reporters](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/16/japan/politics/japan-takaichi-no-warships-middle-east/) yesterday that Japan has no current plans to deploy Self-Defense Forces for escort missions, even as Trump has [asked about](https://apnews.com/article/iran-iraq-us-trump-march-15-2026-9bbed3c906146844be08fdfd02595754) seven countries to join the effort. Takaichi herself warned on Twitter that Japan could face significant crude oil import disruptions starting around March 20 as tankers avoid the strait.

The oil supply concerns are already rippling through Japanese markets and policy. Japan has [begun releasing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/japan-starts-release-of-oil-from-reserves-as-war-snarls-flows) oil from strategic reserves as the conflict disrupts flows, while Finance Minister Katayama signaled readiness to take "bold steps" on currency intervention if needed. The Bank of Japan is expected to hold rates steady this week as the Iran conflict muddles the economic outlook, though Reuters reports the central bank will [maintain its](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/boj-stand-pat-iran-war-muddles-outlook-sustain-rate-hike-bias-2026-03-16/) rate-hike bias.

## Business & Markets

• Japan [started releasing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/japan-starts-release-of-oil-from-reserves-as-war-snarls-flows) oil from strategic reserves as the Iran conflict disrupts supply chains, according to Bloomberg, with WTI crude futures [trading at](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260317/k10015077491000.html) elevated levels on supply concerns.

• Japanese stocks face earnings pressure as the Iran conflict lifts oil costs, Reuters reports, while Finance Minister Katayama [flagged readiness](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/japan-finance-minister-flags-readiness-take-decisive-steps-forex-2026-03-16/) for decisive forex intervention if currency volatility intensifies.

• Vietnam has [asked Japan](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/vietnam-asks-japan-south-korea-help-crude-oil-access-2026-03-16/) and South Korea for help securing crude oil access following the Hormuz shipping disruptions, Reuters reports.

• The Bank of Japan will [likely hold](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/boj-stand-pat-iran-war-muddles-outlook-sustain-rate-hike-bias-2026-03-16/) rates steady this week as the Iran war complicates the economic outlook, though Reuters reports policymakers will maintain their rate-hike bias.

## Government & Policy

• Foreign Minister Motegi [told Secretary](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72377) of State Rubio that Japan will work with the US and others for safe passage through Hormuz, Kyodo reports, though without committing to military escorts.

• Two people died when boats carrying students on a peace learning tour [capsized off](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72381) the Henoko base construction site in Okinawa, according to Kyodo.
