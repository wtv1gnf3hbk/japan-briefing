Japan is grappling with energy security pressures as the Middle East crisis enters its third week. The government announced it will [begin releasing](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260316/k10015076451000.html) oil from private stockpiles starting today, as tanker availability drops sharply due to the Iran conflict. This comes as President Trump has called on allies to send warships to protect the Strait of Hormuz, though Japan says [dispatching ships](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/japan-says-dispatching-ships-to-middle-east-faces-high-hurdles) faces "high hurdles," according to Bloomberg.

The oil release decision reflects mounting concern about supply disruptions, with the International Energy Agency saying emergency stocks will [flow immediately](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/iea-says-oil-from-emergency-stocks-to-flow-immediately-in-asia) across Asia. Reuters reports that [Asia-Pacific allies](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/asia-pacific-allies-ink-57-billion-deals-with-us-companies-burgum-says-2026-03-15) have inked $57 billion in deals with US companies as Washington pushes "Buy American" policies. Defense Minister Koizumi held talks with his US counterpart, with both sides agreeing on [close coordination](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260316/k10015076681000.html) on Iran developments, per NHK.

Meanwhile, Japan's diplomatic evacuation efforts continue, with Defense Minister Takaichi Sanae tweeting gratitude to South Korea after a Korean military transport plane brought three Japanese nationals home from Riyadh. A government charter flight carrying 222 Japanese evacuees from Gulf states recently arrived at Narita.

**Business & Markets**

• ANA Holdings is planning to [launch a](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/business/companies/20260316-316712/) small drone business, the Yomiuri reports, with models designed to inspect infrastructure after disasters as the airline diversifies beyond passenger services.

• Japanese and South Korean nuclear operators secured [US Ex-Im Bank](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/japan-south-korea-nuclear-operators-get-us-ex-im-bank-support) support, Bloomberg reports, as Washington backs allied energy projects following Middle East supply disruptions.

• Nuclear fusion developments are accelerating, with a developer targeting [reactor completion](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/science-nature/science/20260316-316723/) by 2038 for 100,000-kilowatt generation, according to the Yomiuri.

• Energy sector volatility continues as some shipowners are [braving mines](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/03/15/profits-shipowners-mines-missiles-oil-iran/) and missiles to smuggle oil past Iran sanctions, the Japan Times reports.

**Government & Policy**

• The new fiscal year budget faces [substantial scrutiny](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260316/k10015076471000.html) in the Upper House, with opposition cooperation uncertain, NHK reports.

• North Korea [launched a](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/north-korea-launches-suspected-ballistic-missile-japan-says) suspected ballistic missile, Bloomberg reports.

**What to Watch**
