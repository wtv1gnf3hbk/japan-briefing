Japan is navigating a delicate balancing act as tensions escalate in the Middle East, with Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi returning from Washington just as her government considers a controversial military deployment. Defense Minister [hinted that Japan](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72754) could dispatch Self-Defense Forces for minesweeping operations in the Hormuz Strait if a ceasefire is reached between the US and Iran, according to Kyodo News. The proposal comes as Iran [warns of complete](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/22/world/politics/trump-threat-hormuz-blockade/) blockade following Trump's two-day ultimatum to reopen the crucial shipping lane.

The diplomatic stakes couldn't be higher for Japan, which relies heavily on Middle Eastern energy imports. [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-22/japan-says-not-considering-unilateral-talks-with-iran-on-hormuz) that Tokyo is not considering unilateral talks with Tehran. Meanwhile, there was a rare piece of good news as [AP reports](https://apnews.com/article/japan-iran-war-detention-release-193f389a5c1fbbcf6c17ff4afeb07d17) that a Japanese national detained in Iran last year has been released, according to Foreign Minister statements. Takaichi's return from her Washington summit, where she secured what she called "concrete paths" for strengthening the US-Japan alliance, positions her to navigate these Middle East tensions with American backing.

## Business & Markets

• The [Kashiwazaki-Kariwa nuclear](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/22/japan/kashiwazaki-kariwa-resumes-power-generation/) reactor has resumed power generation, marking a significant step in Japan's energy security push as Middle East tensions threaten supply chains.

• A Kyoto medical firm is [launching personal](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72755) iPS cell storage services, according to Kyodo News, representing a new frontier in Japan's biotech commercialization efforts.

• Consumer spending appears to be tightening as the average [cherry blossom-viewing](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/22/japan/japan-cherry-blossom-viewing-budget-falls/) budget fell 13.8%, the Japan Times reports, potentially signaling broader economic caution among households.

## Government & Policy

• Ruling party officials are [consulting with](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260323/k10015081611000.html) the government on handling a provisional budget proposal, with NHK reporting they will consider the Prime Minister's intentions in their response strategy.

• Iran has [issued warnings](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260323/k10015081541000.html) of complete Hormuz Strait blockade in response to US threats, according to NHK, creating a complex diplomatic challenge for Japan's energy-dependent economy.

## Coverage Flags

International outlets are providing more detailed coverage of Japan's potential SDF minesweeping deployment than domestic Japanese media, with Reuters and Bloomberg offering more strategic context than NHK's brief reporting. The gap between Takaichi's detailed Twitter posts about her Washington visit and limited domestic media coverage of the trip's implications for Iran policy warrants closer examination.
