Japan is scrambling to diversify its energy supplies as Middle East tensions threaten critical supply routes, while also exploring new defense partnerships with unexpected allies. [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/japan-industry-ministry-asks-australia-boost-lng-output-during-iran-crisis-2026-03-14/) the industry ministry has asked Australia to boost LNG output following the Iran crisis, as Tokyo's power utility JERA seeks to hedge growing Middle East risks by securing more supply sources. The moves come as Trump expressed hope that Japan and other nations will dispatch naval vessels to help secure the Strait of Hormuz, [according to NHK](http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20260315/k10015076011000.html).

On the defense front, [Kyodo reports](https://english.kyodonews.net/articles/-/72301) Japan is eyeing the introduction of Ukraine-made drones for its Self-Defense Forces, marking a significant shift as Tokyo explores military technology partnerships beyond traditional allies. This comes as regional security tensions remain elevated, with [Bloomberg reporting](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/north-korea-launches-suspected-ballistic-missile-japan-says) North Korea launched about 10 ballistic missiles toward the Sea of Japan in its latest show of force.

**Business & Markets**

• Japan and South Korea are prepared to coordinate action against foreign exchange volatility, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/japan-south-korea-ready-act-against-fx-volatility-ministers-say-2026-03-14/), as finance ministers from both countries signal readiness to intervene in currency markets.

• The US and Japan have agreed on their respective roles in a potential nuclear power project, [according to Westinghouse](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-japan-agree-their-roles-potential-nuclear-power-project-westinghouse-says-2026-03-14/), as both countries deepen energy cooperation.

• Asian nations are set to pledge $30 billion in energy and mineral deals with the US, [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-13/asia-set-to-pledge-30-billion-in-energy-mineral-deals-with-us), as the region strengthens economic ties with Washington.

• Europe repaid Tokyo a favor by supporting an oil stock release, [Japan's energy minister told Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/europe-repaid-tokyo-favour-by-supporting-oil-stock-release-japan-minister-says-2026-03-14/).

**Government & Policy**

• Japan welcomed Bhutanese Prime Minister Tshering Tobgay to Tokyo for the first time in eight years, with Defense Minister Takaichi Sanae noting they held a summit meeting, [according to her Twitter account](https://twitter.com/takaichi_sanae).
