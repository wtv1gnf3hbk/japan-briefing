Japan is positioning itself cautiously as tensions escalate between the United States and Iran over the critical Hormuz Strait. Defense Minister Yasukazu Hamada suggested Tokyo might consider sending Self-Defense Forces for minesweeping operations in the strategic waterway, but only after a ceasefire is reached, according to Kyodo News. Bloomberg reports Japan has ruled out unilateral talks with Iran about the blockade, even as Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi wraps up her Washington summit with President Trump.

Reuters reports the Hormuz closure is forcing global importers to scramble for alternatives to the vital energy chokepoint. Meanwhile, AP reports a Japanese national detained in Iran last year has been released, providing a rare positive development between the two nations.
