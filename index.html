Japan's markets rallied sharply today as news of potential U.S.-Iran cease-fire efforts offered relief from this week's escalating Middle East conflict. The Nikkei surged 4% following reports of diplomatic interventions, while Japanese oil refiners had asked the government to tap strategic reserves as crude prices spiked. Prime Minister Takaichi raised travel warnings to Level 3 for six countries around Iran, telling Japanese citizens to avoid travel to Kuwait, Saudi Arabia's eastern region, Bahrain, Qatar, the UAE, and Oman.

The conflict's ripple effects hit Japanese industry directly. Toyota announced it would cut production by roughly 20,000 vehicles for Middle East exports as flights from Dubai to Haneda resumed operations after days of cancellations. Passengers arriving from Dubai described hearing explosions and missile-like sounds throughout the night, according to NHK interviews.

Business & Markets

• Japanese stocks rebounded strongly from this week's rout, with Bloomberg reporting the Nikkei's 4% gain helped offset broader market volatility across Asia.

• The Bank of Japan is keeping an April rate hike on the table while monitoring the Iran crisis, sources told Bloomberg, as policymakers weigh geopolitical risks against domestic economic momentum.

• A major Japanese labor union set more ambitious pay increase goals for spring wage negotiations, with the BOJ closely watching these talks as a key inflation indicator.

• Space One's third consecutive rocket launch failed, according to Reuters, leaving Japan without a commercial launch capability as the startup's Kairos rocket flight was aborted.

Government & Policy
